The Problem: It is difficult and time-consuming to plan and cook meals.

The Solution: A cutting-edge meal planning app, designed to address the pain points of decision fatigue and time constraints in meal planning.

Research

Interviews

During the ideation phase of the project, we conducted 5 user interviews to build new personas and to inform the design. We prepared an interview script with 26 questions about how users decide what to eat and their struggles with meal planning. There was a trend of meal planning taking up too much time.

Affinity Diagram

Combining the data we got from the interviews and survey data we created an affinity diagram. We combined the data into 6 categories. The main takeaways being that Users have time limitations, Users struggle with food waste, deciding what to eat, not knowing what to buy, Users would like to spend less and eat healthier and more consistently.

Definition & Ideation

Problem Statement

We believe offering a way to find what to cook plan meals for busy people will empower them to cook more, saving them money, time, and improve their nutrition.

Feature Prioritization Matrix

We came up with ideas to be features for our app. We then placed them into a feature prioritization matrix . The 3 main features we came up with were: Adding and tracking user pantry ingredients digitally - using Camera to scan barcodes, Filtering + searching recipes -based on your pantry ingredients, Bookmarking and saving recipes to a weekly plan

Testing

We conducted 5 usability tests based off of the lo-fi prototype. Based on these testing we got a few main takeaways. we discovered that Most readability issues would be fixed in transition to Mid-Fi, however:

  • The path from the Pantry page to the Recipes (Home) page was unclear.
  • The Home page was not readable as where to find recipes.
  • We should highlight the ingredients being used in each step of the recipe.

Based off of this information we updated our user flow before going into mid-fi.
